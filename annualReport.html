Tithe to budget ratio

The church before 2022 was able to cover over \(85\%\) of its budgeted tithe and offerings, as shown in Figure 1. In 2022, we were only able to cover a half of the budgeted tithe and offerings, but we recovered out budgetted tithe and offerings for the years that followed. Why was this the case?

Although we performed well in recovering over three quarters of our budgetted tithe and offerings, the income streams for the church were put into categories, and the offering growth rate and income-category share were estimated. Over the years, over half of the total income of the church were from gross tithes and offering, except for 2023 where the government support contributed much to our income stream than our gross tithes and offerings (see Figure 2). This is just because there is no gross income and tithe value for 2023. Over the years however, gross tithes and offerings have been declining in growth, except for 2024 which say a significant growth of about 50% compared to that of 2022. Apart from government support and gross tithes and offerings, all other income-categories contribute to less than \(10\) of the total income, with each of the categories increasing or decreasing over the years.

Expenditure

For all years, salaries, allowancs and taxes has been in the top two of the highest expenditure incurred by the church (see Figure 3), followed by Nedre Rommen Mortgage/ financial expenses. The other expense categories has made it into the top 5, but have not been consistent over the years.

The expense category growth rate however shows that the salaries, allowances and taxes as well as the Nedre Rommen Mortgage/ Financial expenses are consistently part of the top 5 expenses that is increasing over the years (Figure 4). Some expense categories like support to districts were incurred in specific years, and there have relatively higher growth rates.
